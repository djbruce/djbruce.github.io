Broadly I am interested in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, as well as their interactions with other fields like number theory and topology. My specific research interests are a bit widespread, but currently I spend most of my time thinking about free resolutions, syzygies, and the geometry of the Hilbert scheme of points.

Here is a list of my publications:

  1. Noether Normalization in Families - (Poster)
    Introductory Workshop: Combinatorial Algebraic Geometry, Fields Institute - August 16, 2016
    poster
    Classically, Noether normalization says that if $X$ is an $n$-dimensional projective (resp. affine) variety over a field $\kk$ then their exists a finite morphism $\phi:X\rightarrow\P^n_{\kk}$ (resp. $\A^n_{\kk}$). By studying the distribution of systems of parameters we show similar results hold when $\kk$ is replaced by certain dimension one integral domains namely $\Z$ or $\F_q[t]$.

  4. Noether Normalization in Families
    Algebraic Geometry Seminar, University of Wisconsin - April 15, 2016
    notes
    Classically, Noether normalization says that any projective (resp. affine) variety of dimension n over a field admits a finite surjective morphism to P^n (resp. A^n). I will discuss whether we can generalize such theorems to other bases like Z, C[t], etc. This is based on joint work with Daniel Erman.

  5. Probability of Systems of Parameters
    Speciality Exam, University of Wisconsin - February 29, 2016
    notes
    Given k homogenous polynomials in n+1 variables what do we expect about the dimension of their zero set? Well a good guess would be that each polynomial imposes a dimension one condition and so their zero set should have dimension n-(k+1). In this talk I will discuss how, over a finite field, we may put this intuition to the test. This is based on joint work with Daniel Erman.
